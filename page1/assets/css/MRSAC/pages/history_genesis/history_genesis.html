MRSAC History and Genesis

MRSAC is a well-established premier institution and recognized as one of the best State Centre in Natural Resource Monitoring and Management by offering innovative, effective and optimal solutions by blending RS and GIS technologies with the known technique.

Maharashtra Remote Sensing Application Centre (M.R.S.A.C) was founded in September 1988 under the administrative control of Planning Department, Government of Maharashtra. It was registered under the Societies Register Act, 1860. The Centre’s matter is managed by the Governing Body with its members appointed by GoM and assisted by Executive committee constituted by Governing Body.

At the inception time, the Centre initiated its work in small laboratory in the premises of Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology (VNIT formerly VRCE) campus, Nagpur, with limited Infrastructure, Instrumentation and Skilled manpower. MRSAC started its journey with four Scientist, four Technical and five Supporting staff, mostly on deputation. From August 1994, Centre started functioning from its new office building from same VNIT premises with increased Scientific, Technical and Administrative staff.

Previously the RS-Satellite Data was available in the form of transparencies (Dia-positives) and Hard copy paper prints and the interpretation was carried out visually using HME (High Magnification Enlarger), LFOE (large Format Optical Enlarger) and Light Table for various application projects. The outputs were manually colored paper maps with symbology and legends done by cartographers.

With progression of time and technology the RS data storage and analysis media changed. Now the RS data is available in digital mode and analysis performed digitally with dedicated software and hardware.

The Centre is keeping in pace with cutting-edge technology in the field of RS-GIS, GPS, 3D-technology, Radar, Lidar, Web-GIS, Cloud Computing. Now the Centre has highly specialized dedicated computer labs such as 3D-Photogrammetry lab, Drone data processing Lab, Web-portal and web-site generation lab. Also, the Central Data Storage Facility (SAN), Continuous Electricity backup unit (diesel generator) and Solar Energy Generation Unit, Dining facility, and an Auditorium with capacity of 250 sitting accommodation are the assets of the Centre.

The Centre has spread its wings by establishing two new branch offices at Mumbai (2013) to keep liasoning with GoM. and Pune (2014) as headquarters of mostly government departments reside in Pune. Hence smooth functioning and timely decision making in the various projects of the state can be possible.

MRSAC is a “Nodal Agency” for developing a Maharashtra Geo-Spatial Digital Database System-(MGDDS) for Govt. of Maharashtra and works in coordination with Planning Department, GoM. Under this, the Centre has created a GCP library with high positional accuracy using DGPS Survey technique covering entire Maharashtra state and utilized for referencing of all thematic layers. A gigantic task of generation of Ortho-Rectification of High-resolution satellite data using GCP is under process.

Since the last three decades of hard work in the RS-GIS, the Centre has a huge repository of various scale thematic databases. These databases follow the Maharashtra Geo-database Design Standards with valid Topology and Domain structure and published to cloud as a Web Services.

The Centre is serving the data through MRSAC Geo-Portal to various line departments for multiple queries, analysis, updating, etc. The Geo-Portal was inaugurated on 20th December 2011 by the Honorable Chief Minister of Maharashtra State. The key is the only unique standard dataset available to various Govt. Departments makes decision faster, simpler, qualitative, feasible, transparent, and effective.

MRSAC is recognized as one of the best State Centre in Natural Resource Monitoring and Management by offering innovative, effective, and optimal solutions by blending RS and GIS technologies with the known.

With self-growing and enriching the state with scientific & technical inputs via recent technologies; using the abundant and wide-ranging digital database, on various themes, the MRSAC re-affirms its commitment towards a better, transparent, vibrant, and implementable E-governed Maharashtra.

Serving the State through RS-GIS digitally …. A move to Digital India Initiative……

From Director's Desk

 Maharashtra Remote Sensing Application Centre (MRSAC) was established in January 1988, as an autonomous body under the Department of Planning, Govt. of Maharashtra. It is a premier institution, dedicatedly involved in serving the state of Maharashtra through use of geospatial technology like remote sensing, geographic information system (GIS), global positioning system (GPS), mobile technology, photogrammetry and the ultra-modern technologies of drone (UAV) and LiDAR for 3-D mapping. MRSAC has carved a niche in the e-governance program of the Government of  India with its use of mobile applications for asset mapping and dissemination of geographically referenced assets on web,  superimposed on high resolution satellite data and decision support system, designed on the geo-portal of each department as  per their need. It's no wonder then, that MRSAC has designed, successfully implemented and served data and value added maps for 26 line departments in a G2G scenario. All this has also been possible due to the vision of founder Director and the subsequent Director's as well who have left no stone unturned to usher in the use of latest technology for the welfare of the state.

               The use of geo-spatial technology is also becoming a mandate for every department by creating awareness amongst the departments, stakeholders, business communities and the masses for the need for transparency in governance. Thus, MRSAC is actively involved in G2G and G2C environment for the mapping, inventory, managing, monitoring, asset mapping, for the "ease of doing work" and for betterment of the lives of the common citizen. Urban applications, agriculture, sericulture, soil, land use, roads, water bodies and rivers, watershed, drainages, forest, geology and mining, archeology and conservation of heritage forts, ecology etc., are some of the domains in which MRSAC has made special contribution.  MRSAC has been recognized for its contribution by the Government through the geo-spatial awards for better e-governance for past two years. The newly built branch offices of MRSAC at Mumbai and Pune cater to the needs of Mantralaya and the various department headquarters for laisoning between various departments for the use of geo-spatial technology. It has been my privilege to serve the state of Maharashtra as the Director of this premier institute, ably supported by my handful of core scientists, each with a domain expertise, and usher in the new technologies of UAV and LiDAR and   photogrammetry and 3 D mapping. With the support of the Government, MRSAC has taken up the ambitious project of MahaBHUMI wherein a complete database of resources shall be prepared using very high-resolution satellite data from World View2 and Plaedias with resolution of 30cm in urban areas and 50 cm in rural areas. I am sure that MRSAC shall be able to complete this project on time and provide a very detailed base to the state. I, would conclude by stating that executing MahaBHUMI project would be “ a small step by MRSAC, a giant step for humanity for our State"

Vision And Mission

  • Serving human beings through state-of-art technologies in Remote Sensing and Geographical Information System.
  • Act as catalysts through remote sensing and GIS to increase the responsiveness and efficiency of the line departments of the State.
  • Harness the immense potential of Remote Sensing & GIS for the development and utilization of natural resources of Maharashtra state.
  • To provide proactive support and assistance to Maharashtra state in achieving the e-governance
